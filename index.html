COMP0078 Supervised Learning

The course covers a range of supervised classification algorithms from theoretical and practical computing approaches: Linear regressions, K-NN, Kernelisation, Decision trees, Multi-task learning and Online learning, etc.

What I enjoyed: rigorous proofs and thought process for bias and variance trade off, boosting convergence and Hedge bound.

In the future: I wish to be able to formulate and solve problems in theoretical and practical ways.

Programming Languages: Matlab

COMP0080 Graphical Models

The course covers directed and undiredcted graphical models, HMM, Junction Trees as well as probabilistic inference algorithms for aforementioned models, such as, forward-backward recursion, sum-product algorithm, naive Bayes, EM algorithm and Baum-Welch algorithm.

What I enjoyed: employing Bayes' theorem and constructing graphical representations of an event allow us to infer and to learn the most probable unobservable past, current and future.

In the future: I wish to obtain maturity in Bayesian inference and be able to apply and to advance it in real-world applications, hence contributing to the discovery of the unobservable truth.

Programming Languages: Julia, Matlab

What I enjoyed: As a engineering student with some neuroscience background, I enjoyed to discover the parallelism in functionality between neural networks and our brain. Using mathematics and computations, the former is approaching ATP-powered brain year by year.
